FAQ

Honest answers first

Can Nordfara get me a European visa or residence permit?
Not directly, and honesty about that matters: the firm's license is American. What Nordfara provides is pathway consulting — mapping which countries' self-employment, investor, employment, or remote-work routes realistically fit your profile — and coordination with qualified local counsel in the destination country, who execute the filing under their own law.
Which European countries are realistic for Americans?
It depends on what you bring — remote income, a business, capital, employment prospects, or ancestry. The Dutch-American Friendship Treaty gives US citizens an unusually accessible self-employment route to the Netherlands; other countries run digital-nomad, founder, and passive-income permits with very different math. The right answer is a comparison, made against your actual finances and timeline.
What does the engagement look like?
A structured assessment of your profile; a written pathway map comparing realistic destination options with requirements, costs, and sequencing; and, if you proceed, coordination with vetted local counsel who handle the foreign filing itself. One point of contact throughout.
Does the firm also handle the US side of a move abroad?
US-side questions that travel with you — maintaining or abandoning permanent residence, immigration questions for family members, and the mechanics of returning later — are within the US practice.
