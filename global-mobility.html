US employment immigration, end to end

From the first assignment conversation to permanent residence — visa strategy, USCIS filings, and status maintenance for employers and the people they move.

Book a consultation

Employment immigration rewards early, honest planning. The right category depends on the person's nationality and history, the corporate relationship, and the clock — and choosing wrong costs months. Nordfara's practice is built around getting that first decision right, then executing filings that anticipate the questions officers actually ask.

H-1B

Specialty occupations

The workhorse category for degreed professionals — and the most calendar-sensitive. We handle registration and cap strategy, complete petitions designed to minimize requests for evidence, amendments and extensions, and portability when talent changes employers.

  • Cap registration strategy and timing
  • Cap-exempt filings for universities and affiliated nonprofits
  • Wage-level and specialty-occupation analysis before filing, not after an RFE
L-1

Intracompany transfers

For executives, managers, and specialized-knowledge employees moving within a corporate family — including new-office petitions that put the first employee on US soil. Often the cleanest route for European headquarters expanding into the American market.

  • L-1A executives and managers; L-1B specialized knowledge
  • New-office L-1s with business plans that hold up
  • Qualifying-relationship analysis for complex corporate structures
E-1 / E-2

Treaty traders and investors

Nationals of treaty countries — including most of Europe — can trade or invest their way to US work status without a lottery. For many European founders and family businesses, the E-2 is the most underused door into the American market.

  • E-2 investor cases: substantiality, marginality, and source-of-funds records built properly
  • E-1 trade cases for companies with existing US commerce
  • Employee E visas for executives and essential-skills staff of treaty companies
EB / PERM

Employment-based permanent residence

The long game, planned from day one: PERM labor certification run cleanly, EB-1/EB-2/EB-3 strategy matched to credentials and priority-date realities, and adjustment of status coordinated so employees keep working and traveling.

  • PERM recruitment conducted to withstand audit
  • EB-1 and NIW analysis for standout credentials before defaulting to slower routes
  • Priority-date planning across categories and countries of chargeability

FAQ

Common questions

Which visa is right for our employee?
It depends on nationality, role, corporate structure, and timeline. Treaty nationals often have E-1/E-2 options that avoid the H-1B lottery entirely; employees with a year of service abroad may fit L-1; degreed specialists fit H-1B. The first consultation maps every viable route with realistic timelines, so you choose with full information.
How long does an employment visa take?
Timelines vary by category and by government processing service levels, including premium processing where available. Some treaty and transfer cases move in weeks; lottery-dependent and green-card processes run months to years. We plan start dates around the realistic calendar, not the optimistic one.
Can you work with our HR or global mobility team in Europe?
Yes — that is the practice's core design. We work in European time zones when needed, provide document checklists your HR team can actually complete, and give assignment-planning input before the offer letter is signed, when options are widest.
Do you handle the green card after the work visa?
Yes. Employment-based permanent residence — PERM labor certification and the EB categories — is planned from the first filing so no status time is wasted and key employees see a path worth staying for.
What does it cost?
Fees are fixed per filing and set out in writing before engagement, along with government fees, so budgeting is predictable. No estimate is given before understanding the case — and no case is taken without a written scope.
