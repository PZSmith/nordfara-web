Europe Relocation · Netherlands

DAFT, explained honestly

The Dutch-American Friendship Treaty gives US citizens one of Europe's most accessible self-employment residence routes. Accessible is not the same as automatic.

Book a consultation

A 1956 treaty most Americans have never heard of lets US citizens establish themselves in the Netherlands as entrepreneurs on terms far gentler than the standard Dutch self-employment test — no points system, a modest capital requirement, and renewals tied to the business staying real. It has become the quiet favorite of American freelancers, consultants, and founders heading to Europe.

Scope. Nordfara provides pathway planning and coordination for DAFT moves; Dutch filings are executed by qualified local counsel in the Netherlands. This page is general information, not legal advice on Dutch law.

FAQ

DAFT questions, answered plainly

What does DAFT actually require?
In broad terms: US citizenship, a real business activity in the Netherlands, and a modest capital investment (historically around €4,500) maintained in the business, plus registrations and local formalities. Requirements and practice evolve — the current details are confirmed with Dutch counsel as part of the engagement.
Can I work remotely for US clients under DAFT?
The permit is a self-employment route: freelancers and founders serving US clients from the Netherlands are its classic users. Structuring the activity properly — and understanding the tax residency consequences — is exactly what the pathway plan covers.
Does Nordfara file the Dutch application?
No — Dutch filings are executed by qualified Dutch counsel or advisors, whom we help you select. Nordfara builds the pathway plan, sequences the steps, prepares you for what the filing requires, and stays coordinated through decision.
What about my family?
Spouses and minor children generally have accompanying routes with their own conditions, including work rights that are often broader than the American assumptions. The family picture is part of the plan from day one.
