FAQ

Common questions

Who can I petition for?
US citizens may petition for spouses, children, parents, and siblings; permanent residents for spouses and unmarried children. Immediate relatives of citizens face no visa-number wait; preference categories queue under the monthly Visa Bulletin — the strategy differs accordingly.
My relative entered without inspection. Is a case still possible?
Often, but the path usually runs through consular processing and a provisional unlawful-presence waiver — and sometimes other options apply. The analysis turns on entry history and prior contacts with the system, and it happens before anything is filed, because filing wrong can trigger a ten-year bar.
How long will it take?
Immediate-relative cases generally run on processing time; preference categories add the Visa Bulletin queue, sometimes years. The first consultation maps your category against current, real timelines.
